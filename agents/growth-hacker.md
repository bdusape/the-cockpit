## Knowledge Base

### Platform Algorithm Deep Dives

**Twitter/X Algorithm (2025):**
- **Ranking Signals (in order of importance):**
  1. Engagement rate (likes, retweets, replies, bookmarks) in first 60 minutes
  2. Author authority (follower count, verification, engagement history)
  3. Recency (newer posts prioritized)
  4. Media richness (images/videos get 2-3x more reach)
  5. Thread engagement (replies to your own tweets boost thread visibility)
  6. Link clicks (but links reduce initial reach)

- **What Works:**
  - Threads (3-7 tweets) outperform single tweets
  - Polarizing takes or bold claims (create engagement)
  - Personal stories with vulnerability
  - Data-driven insights with visuals
  - Asking questions that invite replies
  - Posting at 9-11 AM ET, 1-3 PM ET on weekdays

- **What Hurts:**
  - Too many hashtags (max 2)
  - External links in first tweet (save for reply)
  - Posting too frequently (4+ times/day)
  - Low engagement in first hour (algorithm buries)

**LinkedIn Algorithm (2025):**
- **Ranking Signals (in order of importance):**
  1. Dwell time (how long people spend reading your post)
  2. Engagement rate (comments > shares > likes)
  3. Author authority (connections, engagement history)
  4. Relevance to reader (based on industry, interests)
  5. Recency (first 2 hours are critical)

- **What Works:**
  - Long-form posts (1,200-2,000 characters)
  - Personal stories with business lessons
  - Contrarian takes backed by data
  - Document-style carousels (PDFs with insights)
  - Asking thought-provoking questions
  - Posting at 7-9 AM ET, 12-1 PM ET, 5-6 PM ET on weekdays

- **What Hurts:**
  - External links (use them in comments instead)
  - Too many hashtags (max 3-5)
  - Clickbait or "engagement bait"
  - Posting on weekends (50% less reach)

**TikTok Algorithm (2025):**
- **Ranking Signals (in order of importance):**
  1. Completion rate (% who watch to the end)
  2. Re-watch rate (do people watch multiple times?)
  3. Engagement rate (shares > comments > likes)
  4. Watch time (total seconds watched)
  5. User interactions (do they follow, visit profile?)

- **What Works:**
  - Hook in first 1-3 seconds (pattern interrupt)
  - Short videos (15-30 seconds have highest completion)
  - Trend participation (sounds, formats, challenges)
  - Educational content (how-to, tips, tutorials)
  - Behind-the-scenes or "day in the life"
  - Posting 1-3 times/day for growth

- **What Hurts:**
  - Long videos (unless extremely engaging)
  - Low-quality video/audio
  - Watermarks from other platforms
  - Too much text overlay
  - Not using trending sounds

**YouTube Algorithm (2025):**
- **Ranking Signals (in order of importance):**
  1. Click-through rate (CTR) on thumbnail + title
  2. Average view duration (% of video watched)
  3. Session watch time (do they watch more videos after?)
  4. Engagement (likes, comments, shares)
  5. Subscriber growth from video

- **What Works:**
  - Curiosity-driven thumbnails + titles
  - Hook in first 10 seconds (tease the payoff)
  - Pattern of: Hook → Context → Value → CTA
  - 8-12 minute videos (optimal for ads + retention)
  - Consistent posting schedule (weekly minimum)
  - Searchable topics (answer specific questions)

- **What Hurts:**
  - Clickbait without delivering (viewers leave fast)
  - Long intros (lose viewers before value)
  - Irregular posting (algorithm deprioritizes)
  - Poor thumbnails (low CTR = low reach)

**Instagram Algorithm (2025):**
- **Ranking Signals (in order of importance):**
  1. Engagement rate (saves > shares > comments > likes)
  2. Relationship (do you engage with this account regularly?)
  3. Timeliness (newer posts prioritized)
  4. Usage frequency (how often user opens Instagram)
  5. Following count (fewer = more of your content shown)

- **What Works:**
  - Reels (highest reach, 10-30 seconds optimal)
  - Carousel posts (swipe = engagement signal)
  - Educational content (tips, tutorials, how-tos)
  - Personal stories (vulnerable, relatable)
  - Asking questions in captions (drive comments)
  - Posting at 11 AM - 1 PM ET, 7-9 PM ET

- **What Hurts:**
  - Reposted TikToks (algorithm can detect)
  - Too many hashtags (30 max, but 3-5 is better)
  - Low-quality images/videos
  - External links (use link in bio instead)

### Virality Patterns

**Common Viral Formats:**

1. **The Bold Claim**
   - Example: "Most [industry] advice is wrong. Here's what actually works:"
   - Why it works: Creates curiosity gap, contrarian angle invites engagement

2. **The Data Drop**
   - Example: "I analyzed 500 [X] and found these 5 patterns:"
   - Why it works: Social proof, specific numbers, promises actionable insights

3. **The Personal Story**
   - Example: "6 months ago I had $0. Today I hit $10k MRR. Here's what I learned:"
   - Why it works: Relatable journey, vulnerability, clear transformation

4. **The Myth Buster**
   - Example: "Everyone says [common belief]. But the data says otherwise:"
   - Why it works: Challenges assumptions, creates debate, provides new perspective

5. **The How-To**
   - Example: "How I [achieved result] in [timeframe]:"
   - Why it works: Specific, actionable, promises replicable results

6. **The Listicle**
   - Example: "7 [things] that changed my [outcome]:"
   - Why it works: Easy to skim, promises multiple insights, clear structure

7. **The Thread/Carousel**
   - Example: "10 lessons from [experience]:"
   - Why it works: Keeps attention, invites scrolling, high engagement per piece

**Viral Hook Templates:**

- "I spent [X hours/dollars] so you don't have to. Here's what I learned:"
- "Most people get [topic] wrong. Here's the truth:"
- "If I could go back [timeframe], I'd do these [number] things differently:"
- "[Big number] [metric] later, here's what actually worked:"
- "Nobody talks about [overlooked thing]. But it's the secret to [outcome]:"
- "I asked [X people] about [topic]. The answers shocked me:"

### Content Testing Framework

**A/B Testing Methodology:**

1. **Identify Variables to Test:**
   - Hook (first line/sentence)
   - Format (thread vs single post, carousel vs single image)
   - Length (short vs long)
   - Visuals (with image vs without, video vs static)
   - Tone (vulnerable vs authoritative, casual vs professional)
   - Time of day (morning vs afternoon vs evening)
   - Day of week (weekday vs weekend)

2. **Test One Variable at a Time:**
   - Post same content with different hooks (2-3 variations)
   - Compare engagement rates after 24 hours
   - Winner becomes new control, test next variable

3. **Metrics to Track:**
   - **Reach:** How many people saw it
   - **Engagement rate:** (Likes + comments + shares) / Reach
   - **Click-through rate:** Clicks / Reach (if CTA included)
   - **Follower growth:** New followers from this post
   - **Conversion rate:** If driving to product/landing page

4. **Sample Size & Frequency:**
   - Need at least 1,000 impressions to validate results
   - Test 2-3 variations minimum
   - Run tests consistently (same day/time if possible)

### Data Collection System

1. **Pre-Post Data:**

2. **Post-Post Data (24 hours later):**
   - Impressions/Reach

   - Click-through rate (if applicable)
   - Follower growth
   - Comments sentiment (positive/negative/neutral)

3. **Post-Post Data (7 days later):**
   - Total impressions
   - Total engagement
   - Long-tail performance (still getting engagement?)

4. **Qualitative Notes:**
   - What surprised you?
   - What feedback did you get?
   - What would you change?
   - Ideas for follow-up content
